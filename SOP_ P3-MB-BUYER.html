üéØ Role Purpose & Context

Why This Role Exists

The Media Buyer Lead is the execution guardian of the CaniCol v4.0 lean-volume testing model. You are the only role that deploys actual ad spend against products, which makes you the final economic gatekeeper between research/creative hypotheses and real market validation.

Your mission is to:

  • Run 45-60 P1/P2 tests per month within a test bank of ~$4,000-$5,500
  • Maintain strict portfolio discipline: expect 70-80% to fail at P1, 10-16% to reach P2, and only 4-6% to pass P2
  • Protect the economics through ruthless kill discipline ‚Äì cut failing products fast to preserve capital for winners
  • Scale the 2-4 P2_PASS winners per month into P3 profitably while managing risk

How This Role Supports CaniCol Economics

The entire DriveOn Commerce model depends on:

  1. Volume √ó Velocity: Testing enough products fast enough to find statistical winners (45-60/month is the designed throughput)
  2. Budget Discipline: Never exceeding $120 total P1+P2 per product (standard band $80-$100) ensures we can afford the volume
  3. Hit Rate Realism: Accepting that 94-96% of products fail means your job is to identify and kill losers fast, not to "save" marginal products
  4. Winner Focus: The 4-6% P2_PASS products generate all profits ‚Äì your scaling decisions on these products determine company success
‚öñÔ∏è Canon Principle: Portfolio > Product

You are measured on portfolio outcomes (P2_PASS rate, test bank efficiency, aggregate ROAS), not individual product wins. Every dollar spent on a marginal test is a dollar not available for a potential winner. Kill discipline protects portfolio health.

Connection to Holdings & Brand

As defined in L0-02 Product and Brand Architecture, DriveOn Commerce operates as the "Dropshipping Engine" under DriveOn Holdings. Your testing work feeds three lanes:

  • Validation/Micro Stores: Fast 1-page stores for P1/P2 tests (your primary lane)
  • Branded Niche Stores: P3_WINNER and TIER_A_WINNER products that justify brand investment
  • General/SEO Store: Long-term catalog play for proven products with SEO potential

You don't decide which lane a product enters ‚Äì that's determined by L2-06 Scaling Tiers based on performance data you generate. Your job is to generate clean performance data at each gate so those decisions can be made objectively.

P1 Gate ‚Äì Smoke Test (L2-05)

Evaluation Point: After $35-$45 spend OR ~2,000 impressions

P1_FAIL Criteria (HARD KILL)

Kill immediately if ANY of these conditions are met:

  • CTR < 0.8%
  • CPC > $2.00
  • CTR < 1.0% AND zero depth events (no ATC, IC, or Purchase)

P1_PASS Criteria (ADVANCE TO P2)

Advance to P2 if ALL conditions are met by ~$40-$45 spend:

  • At least one creative shows CTR ‚â• 1.3% OR CPC ‚â§ $1.20
  • At least 1 depth event (ATC, IC, or Purchase)
  • Minimum spend of $30 (system has had a real shot)

P1_BORDERLINE (OPTIONAL)

Tag as P1_BORDERLINE when CTR is 1.0-1.3% but depth events are weak. Decision: either re-test later with new creative angle OR kill if pipeline is full.

Workflow 1: Launch P1 Smoke Test

Trigger

R3 product packet marked "Ready for P1" in ClickUp with creative pack from P2-CR

Prerequisites

  • Product has P1-ready creative pack (4-6 assets minimum, at least 3 videos)
  • Store page is live and functional (coordinate with P3-SB-VALIDATION)
  • Current P1 WIP < 10
  • Test bank has available budget

Inputs

  • ClickUp: Product task in "P1 Launch Queue" list
  • Notion Product OS: Product record with AOV, margin data, supplier info
  • Creative Assets: Located in Google Drive /Products/[Product_ID]/Creatives/P1/
  • Store URL: Validation store page URL from P3-SB-VALIDATION

Steps

  1. Pre-Launch Verification
    • Confirm store page loads correctly and has basic product info
    • Verify Meta Pixel / TikTok Pixel is firing on product page
    • Test checkout flow manually (at least to payment screen)
  2. Campaign Structure Setup (Meta)
    • Campaign Level: Create new campaign "[Product_ID] P1 Smoke Test"
    • Objective: Sales (Purchase conversion)
    • Campaign Budget: $45 (lifetime budget)
    • Ad Set Level: Single broad ad set "P1 Test - US Broad"
      • Location: United States (or primary market per product)
      • Age: 18-65+ (unless product-specific narrowing required)
      • Gender: All (unless product-specific)
      • Detailed Targeting: Broad (no detailed targeting for P1)
      • Placements: Automatic placements (Feed, Stories, Reels)
    • Ad Level: Upload all 4-6 creatives as separate ads
      • Naming: [Product_ID]_P1_Creative_01, 02, etc.
      • Destination: Product page URL with UTM parameters
      • Copy: Use hooks from P2-CR creative brief
  3. Campaign Structure Setup (TikTok, if applicable)
    • Campaign: "[Product_ID] P1 Test TikTok"
    • Objective: Website Conversions (Complete Payment)
    • Budget: $45 lifetime
    • Ad Group: Broad targeting, 18-55, US, all genders, automatic placements
    • Upload creatives with product page link
  4. Tracking Setup
    • Confirm UTM parameters in destination URL: ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=[Product_ID]_P1
    • Add campaign link to ClickUp task custom field "Campaign URL"
    • Add campaign link to Notion Product OS record
  5. Launch & Update Status
    • Activate campaign in Ads Manager
    • Move ClickUp task to "P1 Active Tests" list
    • Update Notion Product OS: Status = "In Testing", Current Gate = "P1"
    • Set ClickUp due date to 3 days from launch (typical P1 completion window)
    • Post launch notification to #p3-media-updates: "‚úÖ P1 launched: [Product_ID] - [Product Name]"

Outputs

  • Live Campaign: Meta and/or TikTok campaign actively spending
  • ClickUp Status: Task moved to "P1 Active Tests"
  • Notion Status: Product OS updated with P1 start date and campaign links
  • Discord Notification: Launch posted to #p3-media-updates

Quality Checks

  • Campaign shows "Active" status and is spending within 2-4 hours
  • No disapproved ads (if any, fix immediately)
  • Pixel events showing up in Events Manager within 24 hours
  • Budget pacing looks reasonable (~$15/day for $45 total)

Common Failure Modes

  • Ads rejected: Review creative for policy violations, resubmit with edits, or replace with backup creative
  • Campaign not spending: Check bid strategy, increase budget slightly if underspending, verify audience isn't too narrow
  • Store page errors: Coordinate immediate fix with P3-SB-VALIDATION, pause campaign if page is broken
  • Missing pixel events: Check pixel installation with developer or P4-OP-INTEGRATOR

Time Estimate

15-25 minutes per product (experienced operator, pre-verified creatives and store)

Workflow 2: Evaluate P1 Results & Make Kill/Pass Decision

Trigger

Product reaches ~$40-$45 P1 spend OR ~2,000 impressions (whichever comes first)

Inputs

  • Meta/TikTok Ads Manager: Campaign performance data (CTR, CPC, impressions, ATC, IC, Purchases)
  • Triple Whale / GA4: On-site behavior data
  • Notion Product OS: Product AOV and margin for context

Steps

  1. Pull Metrics
    • Total spend (should be $35-$60 range)
    • Impressions
    • Link CTR (%)
    • CPC ($)
    • Link Clicks
    • Add to Carts (ATC)
    • Initiate Checkouts (IC)
    • Purchases
  2. Apply P1_FAIL Criteria (from L2-05)

    Kill immediately if ANY of these are true:

    • CTR < 0.8%
    • CPC > $2.00
    • CTR < 1.0% AND zero depth events (no ATC, IC, or Purchase)

    If P1_FAIL ‚Üí proceed to kill workflow (see Workflow 3)

  3. Apply P1_PASS Criteria (from L2-05)

    Pass to P2 if ALL are true:

    • At least one creative shows CTR ‚â• 1.3% OR CPC ‚â§ $1.20
    • At least 1 depth event (ATC, IC, or Purchase)
    • Minimum spend $30

    If P1_PASS ‚Üí proceed to P2 launch workflow (see Workflow 4)

  4. Handle Borderline Cases

    If neither clear fail nor clear pass:

    • CTR in 1.0-1.3% range
    • Only 1 ATC on 40-50 clicks
    • No purchases but some engagement signals

    Decision framework:

    • Is there a specific creative hypothesis to test? (New hook angle, different format)
    • Is the product strategically important? (Niche expansion, supplier relationship)
    • Is pipeline capacity available?

    If YES to above ‚Üí tag P1_BORDERLINE, send back to P2-CR with specific creative request

    If NO ‚Üí treat as P1_FAIL and kill

  5. Document Decision

      • P1 Spend: [actual spend]
      • P1 CTR: [best creative CTR]
      • P1 CPC: [average CPC]
      • P1 Depth Events: [total ATC + IC + Purchases]
      • P1 Decision Rationale: [1-2 sentence explanation]
    • Update ClickUp:
      • Move to appropriate list: "P2 Confirmation Queue" (if pass) or "Archive - P1 Killed" (if fail)
      • Add comment with key metrics
    • Post to Discord #p1-p2-test-results:
      • Format: "[Status Emoji] [Product_ID] - [Product Name]"
      • P1_PASS: ‚úÖ + "CTR: X%, CPC: $X, X depth events ‚Üí P2"
      • P1_FAIL: ‚ùå + "CTR: X%, CPC: $X, reason: [specific criterion violated]"
      • P1_BORDERLINE: ‚ö†Ô∏è + "CTR: X%, marginal signal, parked for creative revision"

Outputs

  • Decision Tag: P1_PASS, P1_FAIL, or P1_BORDERLINE in Notion and ClickUp
  • Campaign Action: Either advanced to P2, paused and archived, or parked for revision
  • Discord Post: Results notification with rationale
  • Data Log: All P1 metrics recorded in Notion for portfolio analysis

Quality Checks

  • Decision aligns with exact L2-05 criteria (no "gut feel" overrides)
  • Rationale is clear and based on data, not qualitative judgment

5-10 minutes per product (pure evaluation and logging)

Workflow 3: Kill Product (P1_FAIL or P2_FAIL)

Trigger

Product tagged P1_FAIL or P2_FAIL based on L2-05 criteria

Timing Requirement

Kill must be executed within 24-48 hours of meeting fail criteria. This is non-negotiable for test bank protection.

Steps

  1. Pause All Active Campaigns
    • In Meta Ads Manager: select all campaigns for this product ‚Üí pause
    • In TikTok Ads Manager: select all campaigns ‚Üí pause
    • Verify no spend is occurring within 1 hour of pause
  2. Update ClickUp
    • Move task to "Archive - [Gate] Killed" list (e.g., "Archive - P1 Killed")
    • Status: "Killed"
    • Add final comment with:
      • Total spend
      • Final metrics (CTR, CPC, CAC, purchases, ROAS if applicable)
      • Specific kill reason (which criterion violated)
      • Date killed
    • Remove from any active sprint or focus views
  3. Update Notion Product OS
    • Status: "Killed"
    • Current Gate: [P1 or P2 as applicable]
    • Final Result: [P1_FAIL or P2_FAIL]
    • Kill Date: [date]
    • Kill Reason: [detailed rationale with metrics]
    • Total Test Spend: [sum of P1 + P2 if applicable]
    • Archive checkbox: checked (removes from active pipeline views)
  4. Store Page Handling
    • Coordinate with P3-SB-VALIDATION to unpublish or mark product as "out of stock"
    • Do NOT delete store page immediately (may need for forensic analysis)
    • Mark for cleanup in next monthly store maintenance cycle
  5. Creative Assets
    • Move creative files in Google Drive to /Products/[Product_ID]/Archive/
    • Note any winning creative patterns in creative feedback doc (even if product failed overall, a creative angle might be worth saving)
  6. Communications
    • Post kill notification to #p1-p2-test-results:
      • Format: "‚ùå KILLED: [Product_ID] - [Product Name]"
      • Gate: [P1 or P2]
      • Spend: $[total]
      • Reason: [1 sentence citing specific criterion]
      • Key Metric: [the metric that triggered kill]
    • If product was high-visibility or Tier-S consideration, post brief to #p4-systems-ops for PM awareness
  7. Portfolio Tracking
    • Update weekly kill count tracker (for portfolio metrics)
    • Confirm updated P1 kill rate and P2 kill rate are within expected portfolio distribution (70-80% P1 fail, 84-90% overall fail rate)

Outputs

  • Campaigns: All paused, no further spend
  • ClickUp: Task archived with full documentation
  • Notion: Product marked "Killed" with metrics logged
  • Store: Product unpublished or out-of-stock
  • Discord: Kill notification posted
  • WIP: Freed up capacity for new product launches

Quality Checks

  • Kill executed within 24-48 hour window from threshold
  • All spend stopped (verify no runaway campaigns)
  • Kill reason clearly documented and cites specific L2-05 criterion
  • No emotional language in documentation ("this product sucked") ‚Äì keep objective and metric-based

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Hesitation kills: Delaying kill because "maybe it will turn around" ‚Äì this drains test bank
  • Sunk cost fallacy: Continuing because "we already spent $80" ‚Äì L2-05 criteria exist for a reason
  • Salvage syndrome: Trying to "save" products that clearly failed ‚Äì be strict on P2_SALVAGE criteria
  • Incomplete documentation: Future analysis requires clean kill data ‚Äì log everything

Time Estimate

10-15 minutes per product

Trigger

Steps

  3. Campaign Structure Setup (Meta)
    • Option A ‚Äì Extend P1 Campaign:
      • Increase P1 campaign budget by P2 top-up amount
      • Pause losing creatives, keep only winners running
      • Rename campaign to "[Product_ID] P1+P2 Confirmation"
